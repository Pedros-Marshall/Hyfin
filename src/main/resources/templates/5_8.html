Hi, I'm Raj. I work at a bank specialising in Foreign Exchange. For me, FX is the most interesting product due to its impact on all the financial markets, but also the global economy.

In the global economy, currency price movements influence Central Bank policies as well as drive investor sentiment, promote global trade, or curb excessive growth.

As a result, the Foreign Exchange markets are used by many different participants to speculate or manage currency risk. These can include Central Banks, Corporations, professional funds and individual investors.

Central Banks are major participants in currency markets, and keep a keen eye on exchange rates. They can use interest rates and direct interventions to help manage their economies.

They try to balance the effects of currency fluctuations, (for example where a strengthening currency can cool an expanding economy), by reducing exports and foreign investment.

Central Banks can manage their currencies by either intervening directly in the markets to maintain a level, or indirectly by using interest rates to influence global investor sentiment in the national currency.

Although these interventions can have a short-term impact on the market, the sheer size and scale of the FX market means that in the end, the market usually moves to where investors believe the value is fair.

Hi, I'm Sophia, and I use the FX market to speculate on price movements. As a retail investor, I like the large liquidity and easy access that this market offers me.

I use a product called a Contract for Difference (or CFD). Its leverage allows me to access a market that typically deals in large transaction sizes.

I use CFDs to place short term trades on a variety of currencies, all at the touch of a button, from my mobile device or at home. It has opened the market to many individual investors like me.

There are a variety of strategies that you could follow, whether trading on macro-economic price movements, technical analysis, or taking advantage of interest rate differences between currencies (AKA the carry trade).

The FX market allows me the flexibility to put on trades as and when I like to match my investment strategy, in sizes that suit my investment objectives.

Hi, I'm Kimi, I work in the treasury department of a large multinational corporation. The FX market has a direct impact on our company's daily activities as well as our growth prospects.

We both source some of our raw materials from abroad, as well as export our products to a number of foreign countries.

A weakening in our home currency can boost our export sales, but at the same time can weaken our purchasing power for raw materials from abroad, increasing our manufacturing costs.

Conversely, a strengthening home currency can reduce our sourcing costs from abroad, but also shrink our export sales due to uncompetitive prices.

We use hedging programmes to protect ourselves against currency fluctuations, keeping a close eye on market movements and predictions, trying our best to forecast and manage costs.

So as you can see, FX is an interesting market, with many diverse users and factors at play. However, participants will only ever have one of two objectives - to speculate or to manage their currency risk.
